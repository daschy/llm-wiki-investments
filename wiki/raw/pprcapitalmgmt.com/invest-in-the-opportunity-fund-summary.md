## PPR Capital Opportunity Fund II Overview

The page says the fund acquires and develops Tommy's Express Car Washes in high-growth markets and is intended to be a build-and-sell portfolio rather than a long-term hold. It says the portfolio is being built to appeal to institutional buyers seeking standardized, cash-flowing assets.
The FAQ also contrasts the fund with a "Reliant Income Fund" that invests in housing-sector real estate, which suggests there may be another product or a legacy name in the family.

## Strategy And Return Claims

- Investor returns are said to come from both operating cash flow and exit appreciation.
- The projected total return is based on market intelligence from similar assets that allegedly traded at 16x-24x adjusted earnings.
- The page says the fund is pursuing 7 car locations and that Olympus Pines is on track to build a 50+ car-wash portfolio.
- As of the end of 2025, the page says the fund had 3 operational sites and 4 under development.
- The hold time is expected to be 5-7 years, with the first distribution projected about 24 months after funding each site.

## Tax And Investor Claims

- The page says the car washes are eligible for accelerated depreciation and that passive losses of more than 75% of invested capital were realized in the first year a site became operational in prior experience.
- The page says qualified funds, including self-directed IRAs and 401(k)s, are accepted, but also warns that IRA participation could be subject to UBIT.
- The minimum investment is stated as $150,000.
