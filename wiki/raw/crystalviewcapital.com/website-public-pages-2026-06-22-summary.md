## Sponsor Overview

Crystal View Capital presents itself as a real-estate private-equity sponsor focused on self-storage and manufactured-housing communities. The site says the firm was founded by Matthew Ricciardella, has operated for more than a decade, sources 92% of deals off-market, and uses vertical integration across acquisition, management, asset management, and operations.

The site lists contact information as invest@crystalviewcapital.com, 9515 Hillwood Drive, Las Vegas, NV 89134, and 702-541-6379.

## Fund IV Snapshot

The Fund IV page presents a sponsor-stated $200 million target size, $50,000 minimum investment, 15%-20% target IRR, 7.0%-9.5% preferred return, $2 million+ manager commitment, $300 million-$400 million target portfolio size, quarterly distributions, and an approximately 10-year fund duration. It says Fund IV is open to accredited investors and uses a 506(c) accreditation-verification process.

As of the captured Fund IV page, the sponsor says Fund IV had 25 total assets, 2,100+ storage units, 2,100+ manufactured-housing community sites, and $108 million of AUM. The same page says Fund IV had more than $100 million of specific targets identified and emphasizes self-storage and manufactured housing in secondary and tertiary markets.

## Track-Record Claims

The track-record page contains overlapping KPI blocks, including claims of $167 million+ total investor distributions, $350 million+ capital raised, $650 million+ assets under management, 28,000+ units managed, 31 states with investments, 10+ years of operation, and 92% off-market acquisitions.

The capture also preserves an internal KPI inconsistency: page metadata describes $500 million+ AUM and 25,000+ units, while the visible page body includes both $500 million+ / 25,000+ and $650 million+ / 28,000+ variants. These figures should be treated as unreconciled until tied to dated financial records or investor reports.

The same page presents fund-level performance claims: Fund I full-cycle 47.9% gross IRR, 3.5x gross MOIC, 32.8% net IRR, 2.4x net MOIC, $34 million gross profit, and $24.7 million net profit; Fund II operating 22.3% gross IRR with $61.1 million distributions to date, 30 assets remaining, and 5,800+ units managed; Fund III operating 19.8% gross IRR with $63.8 million distributions to date, 59 assets remaining, and 14,400+ units managed.

The track-record page also includes case-study claims, including Southland MHC in Louisville, KY, with a February 2020 acquisition, December 2020 disposition, $7.1 million purchase price, $15 million sale price, 347% actual IRR, and 3.4x actual MOIC; and DRD Mini Storage in Kingfisher, OK, with an April 2021 acquisition, January 2024 disposition, $1.85 million purchase price, $3.4 million sale price, 43% actual IRR, and 2.9x actual MOIC.

## Strategy Claims

The strategy pages describe self-storage and manufactured housing as recession-resistant sectors with stable demand, low operating costs, affordability-driven demand, and value-add upside. Crystal View says it sources off-market assets, acquires underperforming properties, applies professional management, improves infrastructure and amenities, optimizes utilities and expenses, raises occupancy and rents, and strategically exits to capture value.

Several article pages reinforce the same themes: vertical integration, off-market sourcing, active asset management, call-center scaling, utility reimbursement, rent normalization, infill homes, refinancing, 721 exchange/equity-swap tax framing, cost segregation, bonus depreciation, and private real-estate comparisons against public REITs or private credit. These are sponsor-authored marketing and educational claims, not independent diligence.

## Evidence Limits

- The website repeatedly states that it is not an offer to sell or solicitation to buy securities and that any offer is made only by the applicable PPM.
- The website tells prospective investors not to rely on information other than the PPM when considering an investment.
- Target returns, preferred returns, AUM, distributions, IRR, MOIC, cap-rate value-creation math, refinancing outcomes, testimonial statements, and tax-benefit descriptions are sponsor-provided and unverified.
- The static HTML conversion includes repeated navigation/footer text and some duplicated content because the Framer site renders responsive variants into the page source.
- Some FAQ accordion answers were not visible in static HTML; the broad raw Framer search index was not retained after source pruning.
