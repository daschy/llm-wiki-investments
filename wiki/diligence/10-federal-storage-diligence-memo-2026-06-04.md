# 10 Federal Storage Diligence Memo - 2026-06-04

Related pages: [[10 Federal Storage]], [[10FSSAC5]], [[10 Federal Storage Diligence Questions]], [[Self-Storage Industry Benchmarks]], [[Real Estate Syndicate Diligence Criteria]]

Source status: synthesis from sponsor-provided materials in `sources/10fed/`, public SEC Form D filings, 10 Federal public webpages, BBB/Trustpilot consumer feedback, Inside Self Storage reporting, and the repository's public self-storage benchmark summary. This is investment diligence, not tax, legal, or investment advice.

## Short View

10 Federal Storage is not yet investable on the materials reviewed without additional verification. The sponsor has a coherent niche strategy and credible evidence of prior fundraising, acquisition, and exit activity, but the current 10FSSAC5 underwriting depends on unverified sponsor-reported returns, substantial affiliate-fee leakage, unclear debt terms, incomplete property-level financials, and an automation model that appears to create recurring customer-service and access-control complaints.

The best conclusion is "watchlist / diligence hold": proceed only if the sponsor provides investor-level audited or independently reconciled track record, full property-level underwriting, debt schedules, fee caps or clearer conflict controls, and evidence that customer complaints are not impairing occupancy, collections, security, or reputation.

## What Looks Good

- Real operating platform: 10 Federal publicly describes itself as founded in 2010, headquartered in Raleigh, and operating roughly 130+ facilities with nearly 40,000 units across 16 states.
- Strategy has internal consistency: acquire under-managed self-storage, convert to remotely managed/unmanned operations, use centralized software/call-center infrastructure, then aggregate for scale or sale.
- Prior full-cycle evidence exists: sponsor materials report 10FSSAC1 and 10FSSAC2 exits with 25.75% and 34.85% realized IRRs; Inside Self Storage reporting independently describes 10FSSAC2 property sales and earlier fundraising/acquisition activity.
- Current offering has assets, not just a blind pool: 10FSSAC5 materials list 12 acquired properties, 484,346 NRSF, 2,987 units, and $37 million aggregate purchase price, with a larger identified pipeline.
- The target asset class has a reasonable structural thesis: self-storage remains operationally fragmented, and smaller assets can plausibly support value creation through professionalization, automation, pricing, security, lease-up, and expansion.

## Main Risks

### 1. Track record may not be net investor performance

The track-record workbook says IRRs are project-level XIRRs calculated on a best-efforts basis and cautions that figures should not be relied on for investment decisions. That is not the same as audited, net-to-LP, after-fee, after-promote fund-level return history.

Required follow-up: obtain full investor-level cash-flow waterfalls for 10FSSAC1 and 10FSSAC2, sale closing statements, debt payoff schedules, final K-1s/tax reporting, and a reconciliation from project-level IRR to net LP IRR.

### 2. Current raise and deployment need reconciliation

Sponsor presentation materials say 10FSSAC5 has roughly $100 million or more in commitments. Public Form D data reviewed shows separate issuer filings, including initial 10FSSAC5-A sales of $2.58 million as of August 2025, a 10FSSAC5 Feeder sale of $21.175 million as of August 2025, and 10FSSAC5-B sales of $29.03 million as of April 16, 2026. Those figures may not capture all commitments, parallel vehicles, later closings, or unfunded commitments, but they do not by themselves prove the sponsor's approximately $100 million commitment claim.

Required follow-up: ask for a capital-raise bridge by vehicle, including A, B, feeder, operating partnership, parallel company, funded vs unfunded commitments, redemptions, side letters, and committed-but-not-yet-called capital.

### 3. Debt terms are a gating issue

The repo summaries do not include enough debt detail to underwrite 10FSSAC5. Without loan-to-cost, loan-to-value, fixed/floating mix, rate caps, maturities, amortization, covenants, recourse/guaranties, and DSCR stress cases, the 15%+ target IRR is not diligenced.

Required follow-up: obtain a debt schedule for every acquired property and pipeline acquisition, plus a portfolio-level sensitivity at current base rates, +100 bps, +200 bps, lower occupancy, delayed expansion, and exit cap-rate widening.

### 4. Fees and conflicts are heavy

The offering includes a 1.5% management fee, acquisition fees, property-management fees, call-center fees, software/affiliate-service fees, refinancing/listing fees, developer/general-contractor/project-management fees, and a promote reaching 30% after the 8% preferred return and 40% after a 15% IRR hurdle. The PPM summary also says fees may be increased or new market-rate fees introduced by the sponsor.

Required follow-up: model all fees as a percentage of committed capital, deployed equity, property revenue, property NOI, development capex, and exit proceeds. Ask whether fees are capped, whether independent LP consent is required for new/increased affiliate fees, and whether sponsor affiliates are benchmarked by third-party bids.

### 5. Automation strategy has operating-risk signals

10 Federal's own public materials emphasize unmanned facilities, no on-site property managers, centralized call center, and technology-led operations. Public consumer sources show recurring complaints around inability to reach a live representative, gate/access-code failures, billing increases, auction/lien fears, and security/burglary concerns. Review platforms are imperfect, but the pattern maps directly onto the operational failure modes of an unmanned self-storage strategy.

Required follow-up: request facility-level customer-service KPIs, call-answer times, abandoned-call rates, gate/access incidents, delinquency/lien-sale rates, burglary/theft claims, insurance claims, refunds/chargebacks, move-out reasons, Google review trends by facility, and any customer-protection litigation or regulator complaints.

### 6. Property-level underwriting is incomplete

The acquired property list is useful, but the diligence file still lacks trailing-12 financials, rent rolls, occupancy by unit type, market rent comps, capex budgets, expansion entitlements, tax reassessments, insurance costs, local supply pipelines, and stabilization assumptions. Pipeline assets are mostly anonymized, which prevents market-level verification.

Required follow-up: require a property-level diligence package for each closed and pipeline asset before funding, including purchase contract, closing statement, appraisal/BOV, T12, rent roll, unit mix, competitor rent survey, capex plan, debt quote/loan docs, expansion permits, and modeled NOI bridge.

## Key Questions To Send Sponsor

1. Are 10FSSAC1 and 10FSSAC2 realized IRRs net to Class I investors after all fees, promote, debt, taxes, and entity expenses? If not, provide the net investor return table.
2. Provide audited or CPA-reviewed financials for 10FSSAC1 through 10FSSAC4 and current unaudited financials for 10FSSAC5.
3. Reconcile the approximately $100 million 10FSSAC5 commitment claim to SEC Form D sales, funded capital, unfunded commitments, and vehicle structure.
4. Provide all loan terms and debt sensitivity cases for acquired and pipeline assets.
5. Provide current distributions paid to investors and identify whether each distribution came from property cash flow, reserves, debt proceeds, or investor principal.
6. Provide all affiliate agreements and fee schedules, including any right to increase fees or introduce new fees.
7. Provide facility-level operating KPIs that support the automation thesis, including call-center, access-control, collection, lien-sale, and security metrics.
8. Provide litigation, bankruptcy, foreclosure, regulator, BBB, insurance-claim, and material customer-complaint history for sponsor entities and managed facilities.
9. Explain the REIT tax election and why investor materials refer to a single K-1 rather than standard REIT shareholder reporting.
10. Confirm whether Duo Prato Capital or any intended investor entity can subscribe, and provide tax/legal implications for a U.S. C-Corp owned by Dutch BVs.

## Conditional Investment Standard

Consider moving from diligence hold to possible allocation only if all of the following are satisfied:

- Net investor-level prior-fund returns verify materially above market after all fees and promote.
- 10FSSAC5 property-level NOI, debt, and capex support the target return under conservative stress cases.
- Sponsor provides a transparent current raise/deployment bridge and investor reporting package.
- Fee burden is modeled and acceptable, with no open-ended affiliate-fee surprises.
- Automation-related service complaints are shown to be isolated, improving, or economically immaterial.
- Tax counsel confirms the structure is suitable for the intended investor entity.

Absent those items, 10FSSAC5 should be treated as a higher-risk, sponsor-controlled, illiquid value-add real estate investment whose return target is plausible only with strong execution and favorable financing/exit conditions.
