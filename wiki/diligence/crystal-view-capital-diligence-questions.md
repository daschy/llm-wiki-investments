## Gating Documents

- Obtain the current Fund IV PPM, operating agreement or limited partnership agreement, subscription documents, Form D, side letters, fee schedule, waterfall, conflicts disclosures, valuation policy, leverage policy, redemption and transfer provisions, and investor reporting package.
- Confirm whether the public 15%-20% target IRR, 7.0%-9.5% preferred return, quarterly distributions, approximately 10-year duration, and $50,000 minimum match the governing documents.
- Determine whether preferred return accrues from subscription, capital call, deployment, admission, or another date; confirm whether it is cumulative, compounding, current-pay, catch-up-bearing, and senior to sponsor promote.
- Confirm the sponsor's stated $2 million+ manager commitment: entity, timing, class, fee-offset treatment, withdrawal rights, and whether it is true cash at risk alongside limited partners.

## Track Record

- Reconcile the track-record page's overlapping KPI blocks: the metadata says $500 million AUM and 25,000+ units, while the page body also states $650 million+ AUM, 28,000+ units, $350 million+ capital raised, and $167 million+ investor distributions.
- For Fund I, obtain investor-level cash flows supporting the stated 47.9% gross IRR, 3.5x gross MOIC, 32.8% net IRR, 2.4x net MOIC, $34 million gross profit, and $24.7 million net profit.
- For Funds II and III, obtain asset-level and fund-level schedules supporting operating-status IRRs, distributions to date, remaining assets, remaining valuations, debt, occupancy, capex, reserves, and distribution sources.
- For the Southland MHC and DRD Mini Storage case studies, verify acquisition and sale closing statements, debt payoff, capex, interim distributions, investor-level net proceeds, and whether the published IRR/MOIC are gross asset, gross fund, or net investor figures.

## Fund IV Portfolio

- Reconcile the Fund IV page's 25 assets, 2,100+ storage units, 2,100+ manufactured-housing sites, and $108 million AUM against the current asset schedule.
- Build a closed-asset and pipeline table from investor reports with acquisition date, fund ownership percentage, purchase price, debt, current valuation, occupancy, rent roll, NOI, capex budget, business plan, and current variance to underwriting.
- Reconcile public article claims for acquisitions, under-contract assets, refinances, projected IRRs, cash-on-cash returns, and NOI improvements against actual closing statements, loan documents, property financials, and investor reports.
- Confirm whether Fund III/Fund IV joint ventures, seller financing, preferred equity, loans, or inter-fund transactions create conflicts, cross-collateralization, fee layering, or valuation issues.

## Fees, Conflicts, and Operations

- Identify all sponsor, affiliate, and related-party fees: acquisition, asset management, property management, construction, development, financing, refinance, disposition, guaranty, call-center, software, legal, and reimbursement arrangements.
- Confirm whether vertical integration improves net investor economics after fees or primarily shifts operating margin to sponsor affiliates.
- Review property-management contracts, call-center operations, maintenance staffing, insurance procurement, utility-billing practices, resident rent increases, and tenant complaint history.
- Test whether value-add plans rely on rent increases, utility reimbursements, infill homes, capex-heavy infrastructure work, refinancing, cap-rate compression, or institutional exit multiples.

## Debt, Liquidity, and Distributions

- Obtain loan terms for every Fund IV asset: lender, principal, rate, fixed/floating mix, rate caps, maturity, amortization, covenants, recourse, collateral, reserves, and DSCR sensitivity.
- Determine whether quarterly distributions are funded from recurring operating cash flow, reserves, refinancing proceeds, capital contributions, preferred-return accruals, or asset sales.
- Stress floating-rate debt, insurance, taxes, maintenance capex, lower occupancy, delayed infill, and exit cap-rate expansion against the 15%-20% target IRR.
- Verify the article claim of a $50 million line of credit, including lender, borrower, collateral, covenants, use of proceeds, recourse, and whether it introduces fund-level leverage.

## Tax and Legal

- Review cost-segregation, bonus-depreciation, 721 exchange, DST, UBIT, state-filing, and passive-loss claims with qualified tax counsel.
- Confirm investor tax reporting format, K-1 timing, state source income exposure, depreciation allocation policy, and whether non-U.S. or tax-exempt investors face special issues.
- Treat public tax and estate-planning commentary as educational marketing until supported by legal documents and professional advice.
