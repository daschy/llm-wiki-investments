# Private Market Platform Entity Onboarding

Source: [Private Market Platform Requirements Chat - 2026-06-07](../../sources/private-market-platform-requirements-chat-2026-06-07-summary.md)

Reliability: working diligence framework derived from an LLM-assisted conversation and limited public-source spot checks. It is not legal, tax, or platform eligibility advice.

## Structure In Scope

- U.S. LLC owned directly by two Dutch BVs.
- Dutch BVs ultimately owned by Italian natural persons described as accredited investors.
- Potential investments through Nasdaq Private Market / SecondMarket, Forge, EquityZen, and Hiive.

## Eligibility Framework

Accredited-investor status is necessary for many private-market purchases but is only one onboarding gate.

- SEC guidance includes entities whose equity owners are all accredited investors.
- The direct owners in this structure are the Dutch BVs, not the ultimate natural persons.
- Do not assume that the natural persons' accredited status automatically qualifies the LLC. Confirm whether each BV must independently qualify or whether the relevant platform and transaction permit look-through treatment.
- Citizenship alone is not the central accredited-investor test, but residence, supported jurisdiction, sanctions screening, tax classification, and cross-border compliance may affect acceptance.

## Platform And Deal Gates

- Platform account eligibility and accredited-investor verification.
- KYC, AML, beneficial-ownership, sanctions, and source-of-funds review.
- Entity authority, tax documentation, and suitability representations.
- Issuer consent, rights of first refusal, transfer windows, buyer restrictions, and minimum transaction sizes.
- Direct-share versus SPV or fund structure, including fees, custody, reporting, tax treatment, and liquidity.

Public marketing pages indicate that the named platforms serve accredited investors, but they do not establish acceptance of this specific LLC/BV ownership chain. Obtain written onboarding guidance from each platform.

## Preparation Checklist

- LLC certificate of formation, operating agreement, EIN, and investment authorization.
- BV formation documents, current trade-register extracts, and shareholder registers.
- Ownership chart through all intermediate entities to the ultimate beneficial owners.
- IDs, addresses, tax residencies, and KYC materials for controlling persons and beneficial owners.
- Accreditation evidence for every person or entity the platform requires to qualify.
- Source-of-funds and source-of-wealth support.
- Correct U.S. tax form based on the LLC's federal tax classification and the platform's payee documentation rules.
- Deal-specific subscription, transfer, NDA, suitability, and issuer-approval documents.

## Questions For Platforms And Counsel

1. Which Rule 501(a) category should the LLC rely on?
2. Must each Dutch BV qualify independently as accredited, or will the platform look through to its owners?
3. What accreditation evidence is required, and how recently must it be dated?
4. Does the platform support this ownership chain and the beneficial owners' countries of residence?
5. Are direct shares available to the entity, or only an SPV or fund interest?
6. What issuer approvals, transfer restrictions, minimums, fees, and settlement conditions apply?
7. Which tax forms and ongoing tax reporting will the investment generate?

## Decision Rule

Treat platform access as unconfirmed until both entity-level accreditation and cross-border onboarding have been accepted in writing. Treat each investment as separately unconfirmed until issuer transfer requirements, structure, economics, and tax consequences have been reviewed.
