Public REIT data is not directly comparable to private value-add fund IRRs because public REITs are large stabilized portfolios with public equity pricing, different leverage, liquidity, and reporting conventions.

## Benchmark Snapshot

Public self-storage industry data for 2025-2026 suggests stable but muted fundamentals:

- Nareit self-storage sector data showed 2025 total return of -9.98%, 16.34% year-to-date total return as of May 29, 2026, 4.33% dividend yield, and Q1 2026 same-store NOI growth of 0.6%.
- Nareit's August 2024 sector snapshot showed one-year return of 34.72%, three-year return of 5.51%, five-year return of 11.60%, and average REIT implied cap rate of 5.6%.
- Marcus & Millichap's 2026 outlook says hiring and migration trends are pressuring occupancy and rent growth, while slower construction and demographic demand provide some offset.
- GlobeSt's summary of the Marcus & Millichap outlook says national asking rents held flat in 2025 after three years of declines, vacancy rose modestly to 10.2%, and stabilized rents were roughly 5% below the 2023 peak.

## Implications For Private Sponsor Underwriting

- A 15%+ private value-add target IRR is materially above stabilized public self-storage same-store growth.
- Realized private IRRs above 25% are plausible only with strong execution, favorable acquisition basis, leverage, lease-up, rent normalization, expansion, cap-rate movement, or aggregation premium.
- Public REIT same-store metrics are useful as a base-case operating backdrop, not as a direct proxy for a smaller private fund buying under-managed assets.
- Compare sponsor performance claims against net investor cash flows, property-level NOI bridges, debt terms, fees, promote, taxes, and exit assumptions.

## 10 Federal Comparison

10 Federal reports realized IRRs of 25.75% for 10FSSAC1 and 34.85% for 10FSSAC2. Those numbers are far above the public REIT benchmark set. The diligence issue is whether they are gross project-level IRRs or net investor-level returns after fees, promote, leverage effects, and tax impacts.
