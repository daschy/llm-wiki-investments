# Self-Storage Fragmentation Thesis

Related entity: [[10 Federal Storage]]

Source status: sponsor presentation claims from [[10 Federal Storage Slide Screenshots - 2026-06-03]] and [[10FSSAC5 Pitchbook - 2026-05-27]].

## Thesis

10 Federal Storage argues that self-storage remains highly fragmented, creating acquisition and consolidation opportunities in facilities owned by individual or mom-and-pop operators.

## Presentation Claims

- Storage Cafe estimate cited by presentation: REITs own around 8,400 self-storage facilities in the United States.
- 10 Federal proprietary data science claim: more than 70,000 total U.S. self-storage facilities identified.
- Presentation claim: as of 2024, an estimated 88% of self-storage facilities remained in the hands of non-REIT operators.
- Multifamily comparison shown:
  - Individual operators: 20%.
  - REITs: 80%.
  - Claim: apartment market is more consolidated and more competitive for value creation.

## Target Markets

- 10 Federal describes its process as data-driven, using top-down market analysis and property evaluation focused on facility and local real estate metrics.
- Target facility size: approximately 40,000 to 100,000+ square feet.
- Target segment: highly fragmented mom-and-pop owned self-storage facilities.
- Target geography: Texas and Southeastern markets.
