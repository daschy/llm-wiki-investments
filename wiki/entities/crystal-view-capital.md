# Crystal View Capital

Sponsor of [[Crystal View Capital Fund IV]].

Source status: sponsor public-marketing website captured in [Crystal View Capital Public Website Capture](../../sources/crystalviewcapital.com/website-public-pages-2026-06-22-summary.md). No PPM, operating agreement, subscription documents, Form D, audited financial statements, bank records, tax records, or independent track-record verification was reviewed.

## Sponsor Snapshot

Crystal View Capital presents itself as a real-estate private-equity sponsor focused on self-storage and manufactured-housing communities. The site says the firm was founded by Matthew Ricciardella, has operated for more than a decade, sources 92% of deals off-market, and uses vertical integration across acquisitions, property management, asset management, and operations.

The public website lists invest@crystalviewcapital.com, 9515 Hillwood Drive, Las Vegas, NV 89134, and 702-541-6379 as contact details.

## Strategy Claims

- Focus sectors: self-storage and manufactured-housing communities.
- Claimed sourcing edge: 92% of acquisitions sourced off-market through direct seller relationships.
- Claimed operating edge: vertical integration, in-house asset management, expense optimization, utility reimbursement, rent normalization, occupancy growth, infill homes, infrastructure improvements, and professionalized management.
- Claimed thesis: affordable housing demand, storage demand, recession resistance, cash flow, tax efficiency, and potential institutional exit value.

These are sponsor-stated marketing claims. They should be benchmarked against [[Real Estate Syndicate Diligence Criteria]], [[Self-Storage Industry Benchmarks]], and external manufactured-housing comparables before being treated as diligence conclusions.

## Track-Record Claims

The track-record page claims $167 million+ total investor distributions, $350 million+ capital raised, $650 million+ assets under management, 28,000+ units managed, investments across 31 states, 10+ years of operation, and 92% off-market acquisitions.

The same page presents Fund I as full-cycle with 47.9% gross IRR, 3.5x gross MOIC, 32.8% net IRR, 2.4x net MOIC, $34 million gross profit, and $24.7 million net profit. It presents Fund II as operating with 22.3% gross IRR, $61.1 million total distributions to date, 30 assets remaining, and 5,800+ units managed. It presents Fund III as operating with 19.8% gross IRR, $63.8 million total distributions to date, 59 assets remaining, and 14,400+ units managed.

These performance figures are not independently verified. The website does not provide deal-level investor cash flows, audited financial statements, valuation policy, remaining-asset marks, debt details, or a reconciliation of gross project returns to net investor outcomes.

The track-record capture also contains overlapping headline KPI blocks. Its metadata describes $500 million+ AUM and 25,000+ units, while the page body also states $650 million+ AUM and 28,000+ units. Treat those as internally unreconciled sponsor marketing figures until supported by dated financial records.

## Evidence Limits

- The website explicitly says it is not an offer to sell or solicitation to buy securities and that any offer is made only by the applicable PPM.
- The website says investors should not rely on information other than the PPM when considering an investment.
- Public pages include testimonials, case studies, article posts, tax commentary, refinancing updates, and return targets, all of which remain sponsor-provided and unverified.
