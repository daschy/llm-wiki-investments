# PPR Capital Management

Sponsor of [[PPR Capital Foundation Fund]] and [[PPR Capital Opportunity Fund II]].

Source status: sponsor public-marketing claims from [PPR Capital Current Offerings](../../raw/pprcapitalmgmt.com/current-offerings-summary.md), [PPR Capital Foundation Fund](../../raw/pprcapitalmgmt.com/ppr-capital-foundation-fund-summary.md), and [PPR Capital Opportunity Fund II](../../raw/pprcapitalmgmt.com/invest-in-the-opportunity-fund-summary.md). No legal documents, operating agreement, PPM, Form D, financial statements, or independent track-record verification was reviewed.

## Offering Snapshot

- The public webpage presents a current-offerings page and asks prospective investors to schedule a call with Investor Relations.
- The page appears to market two products: [[PPR Capital Foundation Fund]] and [[PPR Capital Opportunity Fund II]].
- Intro language and linked product pages use a mix of "Cornerstone Fund," "Foundation Fund," "Opportunity Fund," and "Opportunity Fund II" terminology, so the exact legal naming convention is unconfirmed.
- The detailed Opportunity Fund II FAQ also references a "Reliant Income Fund," which may be a separate product or an alternate/legacy name for the housing-focused fund family and has not been reconciled.

## Strategy Claims

- Foundation Fund is described as a diversified residential real-estate portfolio for accredited investors, blending non-performing loans, multifamily, and build-to-rent assets.
- Opportunity Fund II is described as a car-wash development fund targeting Tommy's Express sites in high-growth markets.
- The current-offerings landing page emphasizes a 16%+ target over a projected 60 months, while the detailed Opportunity Fund II page says the project is a 5-7 year build-and-sell strategy with return assumptions based on 16x-24x adjusted-earnings comparisons.

## Current Evidence Limits

- No governing documents or independent evidence were reviewed.
- The page set does not disclose fees, leverage, underwriting assumptions, distribution mechanics, liquidity, or realized results.
- The Foundation Fund's two return descriptions need legal-document confirmation to determine whether they are different classes, elections, or separate products.
- The Opportunity Fund II page set contains an investor-eligibility inconsistency: the landing page says qualified purchasers, while the detailed page asks visitors to confirm accredited-investor status and mentions qualified-fund investments.
- The opportunity-page FAQ's reference to "Reliant Income Fund" adds another unresolved naming cue in the housing-oriented product family.
- The market-intelligence and adjusted-earnings footnotes are not substitutes for audited or reconciled performance data.
