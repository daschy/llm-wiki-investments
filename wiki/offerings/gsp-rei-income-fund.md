## Sponsor-Stated Terms

| Fixed return claim | Minimum investment | Minimum term |
| ---: | ---: | ---: |
| 8% | $10,000 | 12 months |
| 10% | $25,000 | 24 months |
| 12% | $50,000 | 36 months |

- Regulation D Rule 506(c), accredited investors only.
- Monthly distributions, described as paid on the 15th business day, or compounding.
- Quarterly financial statements and management review calls.
- Full or partial redemption may be requested after the term; principal is described as returnable within 90 days.
- Strategy: acquire and renovate distressed or value-add single-family homes in Pennsylvania, Maryland, and New Jersey, then hold for rent or sell.

## Interpretation

"Fixed return" is the sponsor's stated rate promise, but its legal enforceability, payment priority, and principal protection cannot be assessed without the governing documents. The public page does not identify the instrument type, obligor, collateral, lien priority, leverage, covenants, reserves, payment source, fees, or investor remedies. The economic risk could differ materially depending on whether investors hold secured debt, unsecured notes, preferred equity, or fund equity.

The sponsor's statements that it has never missed a payment or lost investor principal are unverified historical claims, not evidence that future principal or payments are protected. The stated 90-day principal-return period may be subject to legal-document conditions, extensions, gates, asset liquidity, or available cash.

The webpage reports a CPA opinion that the structure does not generate UBIT for self-directed IRAs. The opinion and supporting facts were not reviewed, so this is not a reliable investor-specific tax conclusion.

## Diligence Priorities

- Obtain the PPM, governing agreement, subscription documents, Form D, amendments, and sample investor reporting.
- Identify the legal instrument, payment priority, collateral, leverage, guarantees, covenants, defaults, and recovery rights.
- Reconcile monthly payments to property operating cash flow and test dependence on new subscriptions, refinancing, or asset sales.
- Confirm whether the return is simple or periodically compounded, the compounding frequency, and whether the quoted annual rate is nominal or effective.
- Review all fees, affiliate transactions, valuation methods, redemption restrictions, and suspension rights.
- Verify no-loss and no-missed-payment claims against financial statements and complete investor records.
- Obtain the cited UBIT analysis and qualified tax advice for the intended investor account and entity.
