## Interpretation

The target return and exit-multiple footnote are sponsor-stated marketing claims, not verified evidence. The page does not identify whether the 16%+ figure is gross or net of fees, leverage, promote, expenses, or taxes. It also does not explain what capital stack or asset type would support the projected 60-month horizon.

The detailed page says the fund acquires and develops Tommy's Express Car Washes in high-growth markets, aims to build a 50+ car-wash portfolio, and expects a 5-7 year hold. It also says the fund had 3 operational sites and 4 under development as of the end of 2025, with the first distribution projected about 24 months after funding each site. Those are sponsor-stated status claims, not independently verified portfolio data.

The FAQ's reference to a "Reliant Income Fund" suggests the sponsor's housing-oriented product family may still include a separate or legacy name that has not been reconciled.

The phrase "potential tax benefits via accelerated depreciation" is directionally plausible for some real-estate structures, but the page does not provide enough detail to evaluate investor-specific tax outcomes. The market-intelligence footnotes are not operating history and should be treated as unverified comparison claims. The detailed FAQ also warns that qualified-fund participation could be subject to UBIT.

## Diligence Priorities

- Obtain the PPM, operating agreement, subscription documents, Form D, and any supplements.
- Confirm the legal issuer, strategy, and whether the offering is currently open.
- Reconcile the investor-eligibility language between the landing page and the detailed page.
- Review fees, leverage, underwriting assumptions, distributions, liquidity, and tax treatment against [[Real Estate Syndicate Diligence Criteria]].
- Compare the public claims with [[PPR Capital Foundation Fund]] and ask for supporting performance records before treating the 16%+ target as credible.
