# Crystal View Capital Fund IV

Sponsor: [[Crystal View Capital]]

Source status: sponsor public-marketing website captured in [Crystal View Capital Public Website Capture](../../raw/crystalviewcapital.com/website-public-pages-2026-06-22-summary.md). No PPM, operating agreement, subscription documents, Form D, audited financial statements, debt documents, property-level rent rolls, or independent track-record verification was reviewed.

| Item | Sponsor-stated term |

| Target return | 15%-20% IRR |
| Preferred return | 7.0%-9.5% |
| Manager commitment | $2 million+ |
| Target portfolio size | $300 million-$400 million |
| Distribution cadence | Quarterly |
| Duration | Approximately 10 years |

All terms above are sponsor-stated public-marketing claims and require confirmation against the current PPM and operating agreement.

The disclaimer page says the website is not an offer, any securities offer must be made through the PPM, and investors should rely on the PPM rather than website materials. It also says IRR and equity-multiple projections do not represent realized Fund results and are approximate annualized estimates based on assumptions. This makes the public Fund IV page useful as lead-generation evidence, not as governing offering evidence.

## Current Portfolio Claims

The Fund IV page says that, as of the captured public page, Fund IV had 25 total assets, 2,100+ storage units, 2,100+ manufactured-housing community sites, and $108 million of assets under management. It also says the fund had more than $100 million of specific acquisition targets identified, focused on off-market deals from retiring "mom and pop" owners.

The article archive includes many Fund IV acquisition, under-contract, refinancing, and operating-update posts. Those posts should be treated as sponsor updates, not verified property-level diligence, until reconciled against closing statements, debt documents, property financials, rent rolls, and investor reports.

## Strategy Interpretation

Fund IV appears to continue Crystal View Capital's self-storage and manufactured-housing strategy: acquire underperforming assets, professionalize management, improve infrastructure and amenities, optimize utilities and expenses, increase occupancy and rents, distribute cash flow, and seek appreciation at exit.

The strategy overlaps with [[Self-Storage Fragmentation Thesis]] and [[Self-Storage Industry Benchmarks]], but the captured website does not provide enough property-level data to evaluate whether the target return is underwritten conservatively or whether projected distributions are supported by recurring operating cash flow.
