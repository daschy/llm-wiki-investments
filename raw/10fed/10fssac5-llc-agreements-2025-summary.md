## Extracted Governance And Economic Terms

- Two authorized unit classes: Class I Units and Class S Units.
- Class I Units are generally non-voting; Class S Units are voting units.
- Class I holders collectively have ultimate percentages of 60%; Class S holders collectively have ultimate percentages of 40%.
- Preferred return is 8.0% per annum, compounded annually, on unreturned Class I capital contributions.
- Secondary return is based on a 15.0% per annum compounded annual hurdle, net of accrued preferred return.
- Sponsor group contribution requirement is the greater of $500,000 or 2.5% of aggregate capital contributions made with respect to all Class I Units in 10FSSAC5 or the parallel company.
- Maximum aggregate capital commitments accepted by the company and parallel company are $150 million, or up to $300 million with Class S approval.
- Additional capital contributions after commitments are fully called are optional for Class I members; if made, additional Class I Units are issued at $750 per unit.
- Failure to fund a called capital contribution can trigger default remedies, including distribution offsets, legal action, interest charges, and non-defaulting-member funding mechanics.

## Distribution Terms

- Operating distributions first go to accrued preferred return for Class I Units, then split 70% to Class I and 30% to Class S until the secondary return is met, then residual amounts follow ultimate percentages.
- Capital event distributions first go to accrued preferred return for Class I Units, then return unreturned capital, then split 70% to Class I and 30% to Class S until the secondary return is met, then residual amounts follow ultimate percentages.
- Tax distributions may be made at management discretion and are treated as advances or prepayments against future distributions.

## Investor Control And Liquidity Notes

- Management can admit additional Class I members and amend the unit exhibit without member consent when admissions, transfers, issuances, or capital-commitment increases comply with the agreement.
- Management may require sale or redemption of Class I Units if ownership creates securities-law, tax, ERISA, reputation, or similar risks.
- ERISA investors have special withdrawal provisions if continued ownership creates ERISA or related compliance issues.
