### The Power of Execution: Driving NOI Growth at Wisconsin MHC

![](https://framerusercontent.com/images/O5uUBfRUUF9XOMrwCuN3fyy71c.jpg)

This case study shows how active asset management, rent optimization, operational efficiencies, and strategic expansion increased Net Operating Income (NOI) by 178% at a 302-site manufactured housing community. It highlights how vertically integrated management and data-driven decisions can drive forced appreciation in real estate.

**What Drives Value in Manufactured Housing Investments**

In income-producing real estate, property value is largely based on Net Operating Income (NOI). By increasing revenue, reducing expenses, and improving operational performance, investors can create “forced appreciation,” meaning value driven by execution rather than market speculation.

At Crystal View Capital, our investment strategy is rooted in a hands-on, vertically integrated real estate asset management approach, prioritizing disciplined execution and operational excellence to drive long-term value.

Acquired just one year ago as joint venture between Fund III and [Fund IV](../fund-iv), this 302-site [manufactured housing community](../strategy) in Wisconsin serves as a prime example of how our vertically integrated management model transforms underperforming assets into high-yield, institutional-grade properties.

**Strategic Rent Optimization & Premium Positioning**

Upon acquisition of this 302-site asset, we identified a significant gap between existing rents and true market value. Rather than a "one-size-fits-all" increase, we implemented a data-driven rent optimization strategy based on market demand that utilized lot differentiation. By identifying and pricing premium lots based on location and size, we captured the full revenue potential of the site while maintaining a competitive edge and high tenant satisfaction.

**High-Efficiency Hybrid Management**

We deployed a remote management frameworksupported by a seasoned local team. This hybrid approach allowed us to maintain institutional-level operational standards across all 302 sites while drastically reducing the traditional overhead costs associated with "mom-and-pop" management.

**Regional Synergies & In-House Maintenance**

Through our dedicated in-house management team, we strengthened our in-house maintenance capabilities and tapped into regional synergies. By standardizing procedures and sharing resources across our existing Wisconsin portfolio, we achieved:

- **Reduced Repair Costs:** Bulk purchasing power and shared labor across regional assets.
- **Faster Response Times:** Improved tenant retention through better, more professional service.
- **Operational Consistency:** A unified standard of excellence across the board.

**Organic Growth via Strategic Expansion**

Value creation doesn't stop at optimization. We have strategically planned the addition of 16 pad sites, which will bring the community to a total of 318 sites. This expansion provides a clear runway for organic growth, allowing us to create organic appreciation through site expansion and density optimization and maximize the property’s density and revenue potential through disciplined development.

**The Bottom Line: Forced Appreciation**

In the last year, the cumulative impact of our disciplined expense management and operational "heavy lifting" has yielded transformative results:

- **Increased estimated NOI:** 178%
- **Exit cap rate assumptions:** 5.25%
- **Total value created:** ~$11.7 million

This Wisconsin asset is a blueprint for our broader portfolio. It demonstrates the power of the Crystal View philosophy: when data-driven decision-making meets execution, the result is measurable, long-term success for our investors.
