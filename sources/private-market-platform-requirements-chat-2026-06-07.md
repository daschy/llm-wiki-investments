# Private Market Platform Requirements Chat - 2026-06-07

## Source Type

LLM-assisted research conversation about private-market platform eligibility and accredited-investor requirements.

## Scope

The conversation examined whether a U.S. LLC owned by two Dutch BVs, ultimately owned by two Italian citizens who are accredited investors, could invest through private-market platforms such as Nasdaq Private Market, Forge, EquityZen, and Hiive.

This note is a source artifact, not legal advice. It should be verified against platform onboarding materials, current SEC rules, and securities counsel before being used for an investment decision.

## Key Conclusions

- Nasdaq Private Market / NPM SecondMarket generally requires buyers to be accredited investors.
- U.S. citizenship is not itself the core issue for accredited-investor status; the key questions are investor status, KYC/AML, tax classification, beneficial ownership, sanctions screening, and platform-specific eligibility.
- A U.S. LLC can potentially qualify as an accredited investor under SEC Rule 501(a)(8) if all of its equity owners are accredited investors.
- In a structure where the LLC is directly owned by two Dutch BVs, and those BVs are ultimately owned by Italian natural persons, platforms may either:
  - accept qualification if each Dutch BV qualifies as accredited on its own;
  - look through the Dutch BVs to the ultimate beneficial owners; or
  - require additional legal/accountant verification before accepting the structure.
- The clean onboarding language is:

> The investing entity is a U.S. LLC. It seeks to qualify as an accredited investor under Rule 501(a)(8) because all direct and indirect equity owners are accredited investors. The LLC is owned by two Dutch BVs, each ultimately owned by accredited natural persons. We can provide ownership documentation, beneficial-owner KYC, and accreditation representations.

## Platform Notes

### Nasdaq Private Market

- Buyers generally must be accredited investors.
- Institutional investors may need to confirm QIB status or accredited-investor status.
- Deal-level eligibility can still apply, including issuer approval, transfer restrictions, minimums, or suitability limits.
- Expected documentation includes identity/KYC materials, tax documents, entity formation documents, beneficial ownership records, and accreditation evidence.

### Forge

- U.S. buyers must be accredited investors to purchase private securities.
- Forge onboarding may include KYC/AML, investor-status representations, and deal-specific diligence.
- Direct secondary transactions can involve issuer transfer restrictions, right of first refusal, board/company approval, and buyer approval.
- The LLC/BV structure may be accepted if the ownership chain and accredited status can be documented, but direct cap-table transfer mechanics may make Forge more case-by-case.

### EquityZen

- Investors can sign up before accreditation, but private placements generally require accredited-investor status.
- EquityZen uses accredited-investor questionnaires, suitability questionnaires, and binding investor representations.
- EquityZen commonly uses fund/SPV structures, which may make entity subscription more standardized.
- The LLC may qualify through the all-equity-owners accredited-investor route, but questionnaire wording may require direct-owner or look-through support for the Dutch BVs.

### Hiive

- Buyers of private stock must be accredited investors.
- Hiive requires KYC, AML, suitability checks, and customer agreement onboarding.
- Issuers may impose additional trading restrictions, approval rights, windows, buyer limits, or transfer conditions.
- The LLC/BV structure may be accepted if beneficial ownership, source of funds, and accredited-investor status can be documented, but issuer approval may still be required.

## "Not Formed Specifically" Clarification

Certain entity-accredited-investor categories require that an entity was not formed for the specific purpose of acquiring the securities being offered.

A cleaner fact pattern:

- LLC was formed as a general investment or holding vehicle.
- Operating agreement allows multiple investments.
- Entity can invest across public and private opportunities.
- Capital is not tied only to one target company.
- Books, bank accounts, and investment records are maintained.
- All equity owners are accredited investors.

A riskier fact pattern:

- LLC is formed immediately before one private-market deal.
- Entity exists only to buy one target company's shares.
- Documentation or name is tied to one target investment.
- Non-accredited investors are pooled through the entity.
- Entity is used to avoid platform or offering eligibility rules.

For an LLC qualifying under the "all equity owners are accredited investors" rule, this limitation is less central than it is for the $5 million assets/investments entity tests. However, platforms may still ask because they want to avoid pooled vehicles created solely to bypass onboarding, minimums, or transfer restrictions.

## Likely Documentation Package

- LLC certificate of formation and operating agreement.
- Dutch BV formation documents, trade register extracts, and shareholder registers.
- Ownership chart showing ultimate beneficial owners.
- IDs, addresses, and KYC materials for Italian beneficial owners.
- Proof of authority for the LLC to invest.
- Accreditation representations from the ultimate beneficial owners.
- Financial statements or bank/brokerage statements if relying on entity asset or investment thresholds.
- Source-of-funds explanation.
- Tax forms, such as W-9 or W-8 series forms depending on tax classification.
- NDA, suitability questionnaire, customer agreement, and deal-specific subscription or transfer documents.
