# Claude Conversation: Real Estate Syndicate Due Diligence

Source: extracted notes from `/Users/1q82/Downloads/Claude.pdf`.

This document summarizes a Claude chat about finding and doing due diligence on U.S. real estate syndicate funds, including the investment setup with Duo Prato Capital, a U.S. LLC/C-Corp owned by two Dutch BVs.

## 1. Due Diligence Criteria for Real Estate Syndicates

- Sponsor/operator track record
- Full-cycle deal history
- Performance through downturns
- Sponsor "skin in the game"
- Litigation, foreclosures, bankruptcies
- Transparency and investor reporting
- Deal business plan, underwriting, market, debt, fees, and reserves
- Legal and tax review:
  - Private Placement Memorandum (PPM)
  - Operating agreement
  - LP rights
  - Liquidity terms
  - K-1 timing and treatment

## 2. Investment Profile

- Accredited investor
- Investing through Duo Prato Capital
- Structure:
  - U.S. LLC/C-Corp
  - Owned by two Dutch BV shareholders
  - Each Dutch BV owns 50%
- Target capital:
  - $160k over 2 years
- Goal:
  - Enough cash flow during the first 5-7 years to cover LLC expenses
  - Most return expected through capital growth
  - Ideal target is roughly doubling capital over 5-7 years
- Focus:
  - U.S. deals only
  - Preference for diversified funds rather than single-property deals

## 3. Explanation of the C-Corp Blocker

- A U.S. C-Corp blocker can shield foreign owners from direct U.S. tax filings and FIRPTA complexity.
- The tradeoff is U.S. corporate-level tax, plus possible dividend withholding when distributions are made to the Dutch BVs.
- The U.S.-Netherlands treaty may reduce withholding, but it does not eliminate U.S. corporate tax.

## 4. Proposed Scoring Rubric

- Sponsor: 30%
- Business plan and market: 22%
- Financials and debt: 25%
- Fees and alignment: 10%
- Tax/legal fit: 8%
- Macro fit: 5%

Red flags included:

- Aggressive underwriting
- Risky floating-rate debt
- Weak sponsor transparency
- Refusal to accept a C-Corp LP

## 5. Channels to Find Syndicates

- Online marketplaces:
  - CrowdStreet
  - EquityMultiple
  - RealtyMogul
  - Yieldstreet
- Direct-to-sponsor relationships
- 506(c) publicly marketed deals
- 506(b) deals via pre-existing sponsor relationships
- Sector-specialist fund managers
- LP communities and investor groups
- Conferences such as Best Ever Conference and IMN/RealShare events
