# Self-Storage Industry Benchmarks Since 2020 - 2026-06-04

Source status: public-market benchmark synthesis compiled on June 4, 2026 from Public Storage annual reports, Nareit sector data, and the repository's broader self-storage benchmark summary. Use this as a directional public REIT operating benchmark for comparing private self-storage sponsor claims. Public REIT same-store operating metrics are not directly comparable to private value-add fund IRRs because they reflect large stabilized portfolios, different leverage, public equity pricing, liquidity, fees, and reporting conventions.

## Contextual Benchmarks

- Extra Space Storage's 2020 annual report cited the Self-Storage Almanac for national average physical occupancy of 92.2% in 2020, versus Extra Space's wholly owned store average occupancy of 93.1%.
- Newmark's 4Q 2020 REIT report characterized the public self-storage REIT sector as ending 2020 at record-high occupancy and reported 12.91% total shareholder return for self-storage REITs in 2020, citing Nareit.
- The repository's 2026 benchmark summary shows a muted recent operating backdrop: Public Storage 2025 same-store NOI declined 0.5%, Extra Space Storage declined 1.7%, CubeSmart declined 1.1%, and Nareit Q1 2026 self-storage same-store NOI growth was 0.6%.

## Diligence Takeaways

- The 2021-2022 period should be treated as an outlier boom, not a normalized underwriting base case.
- A reasonable stabilized public-market benchmark since 2020 is roughly 90%-96% same-store occupancy, with normalized same-store NOI growth closer to low single digits than the 2021-2022 double-digit surge.
- Private sponsor targets such as 15%+ IRR require value creation beyond stabilized public same-store performance, including acquisition basis, lease-up, rent normalization, expansion, automation savings, leverage, fee drag management, and exit-cap-rate assumptions.
