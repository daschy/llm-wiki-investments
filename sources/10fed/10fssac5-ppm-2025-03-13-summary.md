## Extracted Terms

- Offering limited to accredited investors under Regulation D.
- Minimum Class I Unit investment is $100,000, subject to company discretion to accept smaller subscriptions.
- Class I Units are non-voting investor units with certain consent rights; Class S Units are voting units owned by the sponsor group.
- Use of proceeds includes acquisition of inefficiently managed self-storage facilities, conversion from staffed to unmanned operations, and possible distressed debt acquisitions.
- New construction is limited to 25% of offering proceeds unless otherwise approved by non-sponsor Class I holders.
- Drawdowns may occur on at least 10 business days' notice.
- Offering period ends on the second anniversary of the initial closing unless terminated earlier, with a one-year sponsor extension option.
- Manager or sponsor may enter into side letters with one or more investors.
- Units are restricted securities with no public market and limited transferability.

## Fee And Conflict Points

- 1.5% annual management fee.
- Acquisition fee to sponsor affiliates equal to the greater of $50,000 or 2.0% of gross purchase price.
- Property management, call center, refinancing/listing, developer, general contractor, project management, proprietary software, and other affiliate-service fees are disclosed.
- PPM says disclosed fees may be increased by the sponsor if aligned with current market prices and new market-rate fees may be introduced if required to support investment properties.

## Risk Areas

- Speculative private offering; investors must be able to bear a total loss and indefinite illiquidity.
- Competition, future financing availability, key employee dependence, construction cost and approval delays, surety bond availability, technology failure, cybersecurity, third-party vendors, call center staffing, and third-party data-source accuracy.
