# Self-Storage Industry Benchmarks - 2026-06-04

Source status: public industry and public-company data gathered on June 4, 2026. Use as a benchmark set for comparing private self-storage sponsor claims, especially 10 Federal Storage. Public REIT operating metrics are not directly comparable to private value-add fund IRRs because they reflect large stabilized portfolios, public equity pricing, different leverage, and public-market liquidity.

## Public Storage 2025 Same-Store Metrics

- Same-store facilities: 2,565 facilities and 175.3 million net rentable square feet.
- 2025 same-store total revenues: $3.765 billion, roughly flat year over year.
- 2025 same-store NOI: $2.829 billion, down 0.5% year over year.
- 2025 same-store rental income: $3.636 billion, up 0.1% year over year.
- 2025 weighted-average same-store square-foot occupancy: 92.0%, down 0.4 percentage points from 92.4% in 2024.
- 2025 realized annual rental income per occupied square foot: $22.54, up 0.5% year over year.
- Public Storage attributed flat same-store revenue to higher realized rent per occupied square foot offset by lower average occupancy, with softness in demand and lower move-in rental rates.
- Public Storage non-same-store growth was materially stronger: acquired facilities revenue increased 32.7% and newly developed / expanded facilities revenue increased 14.0% year over year.

## Broader Market Signals

- Marcus & Millichap's 2026 outlook says hiring and migration trends are pressuring occupancy and rent growth, while construction activity is slowing and demographic demand from younger and older cohorts provides some offset.
- GlobeSt's summary of the Marcus & Millichap outlook says national asking rents held flat in 2025 after three years of declines, vacancy rose modestly to 10.2%, and stabilized rents were roughly 5% below the 2023 peak because operators used discounts to support occupancy.

## Benchmark Takeaways For 10 Federal Storage

- A 15%+ private value-add target IRR is materially above stabilized public REIT same-store growth and public-market total return expectations.
- Sponsor-reported 10FSSAC1 and 10FSSAC2 realized IRRs of 25.75% and 34.85% would be exceptional relative to public REIT sector returns if they are true net investor-level returns.
- Public REIT operating data suggest the 2025-2026 self-storage industry is stable but not high-growth: same-store revenues were roughly flat, same-store NOI ranged from modestly positive at the sector level to negative at several large operators, and occupancy generally sat around the high 80s to low 90s depending on operator and portfolio.
- Value-add returns depend on acquisition basis, lease-up, rent normalization, expansion execution, leverage, fees, and exit cap rates. Compare 10 Federal's claims against property-level NOI bridges and net investor cash flows, not only sponsor IRR tables.
